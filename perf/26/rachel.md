# Self eval

# **Expanded reach without compromising quality**

## **Doubled traffic coverage**

**Situation**

- Isomer covered [10% of information government traffic](https://reports.open.gov.sg/isomer/metrics/2024-10-01) despite having 55% coverage of sites
- With larger ministries scheduled for migration in 2025, we needed to extend the platform's capabilities strategically
- Agencies wanted bespoke capabilities; building them case-by-case would fragment the platform and increase maintenance burden

**Contribution**

- Expanded core platform capabilities rather than accommodate one-off customisations. I led direct engagements with high-traffic agencies, explicitly [managing trade-offs](https://www.notion.so/Isomer-priorities-and-evaluation-criteria-25077dbba78880ff8ccaffef74bc0973?pvs=21) between custom needs and platform standards. Reframed agency-specific requests into reusable components.
- Delivered critical capabilities required to unblock key migrations, including storytelling components, [scheduled publishing](https://www.notion.so/Scheduled-publishing-21777dbba788800ebafcfee34f35fff2?pvs=21) and [dynamic content via DGS](https://www.notion.so/1f377dbba788803b9eefec9182712341?pvs=21).
- I also ran IA [workshops](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/17li_PlOBjhwy1BV4wwdKDUgjwrZjG1HQB38q8ga_hgU/edit?slide=id.p1#slide=id.p1) (e.g. MFA, HSA) to shift agency mindset from "replicate our old site" to "design for user behaviour”

**Impact**

- Launched 196 sites totalling 60.5M visits, site traffic at 20% (increase of 57% from 12.75% last perf)
- 75% of ministry sites are now on Isomer
- Median scores (Dec 2025): Performance 91, Accessibility 97, SEO 86 (96 for Next), exceeding our 90 benchmark for “good” performance
- ~$6M in annual savings across above sites (~14% of Optical's indicative cost)
- MFA has since engaged us for ongoing content consultations, using page-level data to make evidence-based decisions on site improvements

## **Strengthened cross-product partnerships**

**Situation**

- Integrations with adjacent OGP/GT products were ad-hoc; without jointly positioning ourselves, agencies with complex needs were defaulting to expensive custom platforms

**Contribution**

- **SearchSG:** Established shared roadmaps (e.g. enabling PDF search to support eGazette). Partnered on [joint pitches](https://www.notion.so/26477dbba788809492d0d7c182e4eb46?pvs=21) to agencies (MFA, Temasek Polytechnic)
- **WOGAA:** Secured monthly performance data feeds and [built a performance dashboard](https://lookerstudio.google.com/u/0/reporting/33b9c70b-6e4b-4551-a20f-621de380d7b2) for real-time tracking
- **FormSG:** Leveraged Personalise integration to enable embeddable mailing list sign-up forms
- **DGS:** Scoped and delivered Isomer's first [DGS-powered component](https://www.notion.so/1f377dbba788803b9eefec9182712341?pvs=21), enabling dynamic features on a static platform across 89 MFA sites

**Impact**

- FormSG and DGS integrations directly contributed to winning MOF, ACRA and MFA
- DGS opens warm leads with agencies with more dynamic needs (LTA, Judiciary)
- Able to share [holistic post-launch insights](https://www.notion.so/MFA-Post-migration-review-sharing-30277dbba78880468779e1a8825eb07c?pvs=21); across Search, WOGAA and survey data with agencies like MFA

## **Uplifted performance standards**

**Situation**

- When we consolidated measurement to WOGAA, performance scores dropped due to methodological differences.
- Without alignment on what was “good enough”, we risked score-chasing or, conversely, complacency.

**Contribution**

- Adopted WOGAA as single source of truth, reviewed six months of historical data to map the [performance landscape](https://www.notion.so/Oct-analysis-sites-overview-2a977dbba78880f48805c5be821d7380?pvs=21), and elevated performance as top team priority
- Led [alignment discussions](https://www.notion.so/Jan-2026-performance-update-2fb77dbba788805f97d2cfd11a241f5a?pvs=21) to set a minimum of 75 and a median target of 90, distinguishing between UX-impacting degradation and unavoidable trade-offs from 3rd party tools

**Impact**

- % of Isomer traffic ≥75 improved from 77% (Q3) to 87% (Q4) after [fixes](https://www.notion.so/Isomer-Product-Review-Dec-2025-2b677dbba788800cbd8ec90c5d108f10?pvs=21)
- Median performance improved from 79 (Q3) to 82 (Q4)
- Established a [principled framework](https://www.notion.so/Jan-2026-performance-update-2fb77dbba788805f97d2cfd11a241f5a?pvs=21) for handling exceptions while protecting overall UX quality

## **Embedded security governance**

- **Situation**
  - Large agencies (e.g. MINDEF, MFA) require roughly 2 weeks of back-and-forth with us to compile IM8 compliance declarations (SSP, audit logs)
- **Contribution**
  - Scoped integration of IM8 Reform requirements: Singpass-based [2FA](https://www.notion.so/19077dbba78880e4877fe10655402bb0?pvs=21) and site-level audit logs
  - Owned VAPT lifecycle across Isomer Classic, Next and Go
- **Impact**
  - Reusable Isomer SSP templates on [GovTech platform](https://docs.standards.tech.gov.sg/guides/component-guides/#isomer-next-studio)
  - 2FA enforced for all users
  - Audit logs enabled for 103 sites, reducing reporting time from 52 hours to ~5 minutes

# **Scaled the team’s ability to deliver**

## **Brought clarity to a fragmented roadmap**

**Situation**

- 4 versions of Isomer: Next, Classic, Legacy, eGazette
- Polyglot and GoGov added in Feb 2025
- Team had very distributed priorities

**Contribution**

- Established [long-term vision and evaluation criteria](https://www.notion.so/Isomer-priorities-and-evaluation-criteria-25077dbba78880ff8ccaffef74bc0973?pvs=21) across Isomer priorities
- Established [shared OKRs](https://www.notion.so/42ef0d0ecfee4b04ab931b8828c833d5?pvs=21) to align Polyglot and GoGov within broader Isomer strategy
- Defined roadmap for deprecation of all other Isomer versions

**Impact**

- Team has been consistently using OKRs to measure team performance for 4 quarters
- Team was able to bring focus back to Isomer after Q2 when GoGov was brought to maintenance
- Team is on target to achieve deprecation of all other Isomer versions by end 2026

## **Unblocked migration velocity through self-serve capabilities**

- **Situation**
  - Each migration required repeated Eng and Ops involvement for repetitive edits due to CMS gaps
  - This slowed migration velocity and created unhealthy reliance on Ops
  - We lacked quantitative signals on agency sentiment
- **Contribution**
  - Mapped [end-to-end migration workflows](https://www.figma.com/board/jIqr6BBBe08HRTsdKGrTm0/-Crit--Migration-journey?node-id=0-1&p=f&t=N6UqbgGg8PyYLKoi-0) with Se Hyun to identify high-friction, high-repeat tasks
  - Introduced and [analysed NPS](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1CAbC-F3-DLple2kRt2AK82mRQmv1DwFUcfhqFdTDmcM/edit?gid=128858632#gid=128858632) via Intercom
  - Scoped and [prioritised self-serve features](https://www.notion.so/Isomer-priorities-and-evaluation-criteria-25077dbba78880ff8ccaffef74bc0973?pvs=21) that were repetitive, time-intensive, and safe to delegate
  - Delivered and [managed release](https://www.notion.so/17077dbba78880c4be6ad35b1e486070?pvs=21) for user management, site settings controls, tag assignment, index page controls, and collection link improvements by defining and tracking clear OKRs and active sprint management
  - Updated [Isomer support](https://support.isomer.gov.sg/en/) with guides for all new features, along with [monthly release notes](https://support.isomer.gov.sg/en/collections/13818535-what-s-new-on-isomer)
- **Impact**
  - Migration capacity [more than doubled](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/194RqxljTsVNlv-mrLyWphSxsKwMOr_Wo0cDa4hMZZhE/edit?gid=44151680#gid=44151680) from Q4 2024 to Q2 2025
  - Eliminated ~22 user management requests/month (6 hours engineering saved monthly)
  - Reduced ~4 hours per site from pre/post-launch edits for site settings (132 hours saved in Q3 alone)
  - Index page controls saved ~18 hours per site
  - Established baseline NPS (0 → 2)
  - Created a feedback loop that shifted roadmap discussions from anecdotal complaints to data-informed trade-offs
